### 3. Muslim Scientists & Scholars Section
**Purpose**: Highlight the contributions of Muslim scholars to world civilization

**Scholars Featured**:

1. **Al-Khwarizmi (780-850 CE)**
   - Field: Mathematics & Astronomy
   - Contributions: Father of Algebra, algorithms, Hindu-Arabic numerals
   - Modern Impact: Foundation of computer science and programming

2. **Ibn Sina/Avicenna (980-1037 CE)**
   - Field: Medicine & Philosophy
   - Contributions: Canon of Medicine, experimental medicine
   - Modern Impact: Evidence-based medicine and clinical trials

3. **Al-Jazari (1136-1206 CE)**
   - Field: Engineering & Mechanics
   - Contributions: Programmable automata, water clocks, robotics principles
   - Modern Impact: Robotics, automation, and artificial intelligence

4. **Ibn al-Haytham/Alhazen (965-1040 CE)**
   - Field: Optics & Scientific Method
   - Contributions: Scientific method, camera obscura, laws of optics
   - Modern Impact: Photography, telescopes, scientific research methodology

5. **Al-Biruni (973-1048 CE)**
   - Field: Geography & Anthropology
   - Contributions: Earth's circumference calculation, cultural studies
   - Modern Impact: GPS technology, satellite mapping, cultural studies

6. **Ibn Rushd/Averroes (1126-1198 CE)**
   - Field: Philosophy & Logic
   - Contributions: Aristotelian philosophy, logic and reasoning
   - Modern Impact: Logical reasoning in AI, philosophical foundations of science

**Features**:
- Historical period and field of expertise
- Key contributions listed with checkmarks
- Historical legacy explanation
- Modern impact on current technology
- Unique icons and color gradients for each scholar
